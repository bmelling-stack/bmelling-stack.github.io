What Video Games and Why?

Video games has always been a way to connect with others online while having fun. I think thats the beauty in it for me. Not only is it a way to connect with people, but it was unique because it allowed me to play with my friends and explore all genres of games. Theres literally something for everyone. Personally, I enjoyed playing games where I'd explore new worlds, complete challenges, and compete with other players to be the best. These generes typically included role playing games and first person shooters. So, think of games like Skyrim, Grand Theft Auto, Call of Duty, Halo, Valorant, and League of Legends. While I don't play all of these games anymore, the ones listed definitley took a lot of my time growing up.

Video game list!

  • Halo Series - A first person shooter set in a sci-fi universe where you play as a super soldier fighting against aliens who want to take over the universe.
  • Call of Duty Series - A first person shooter series that focuses on modern and historical warfare with both single-player and multiplayer modes (and sometimes zombies).
  • Grand Theft Auto Series - An open-world action-adventure game where players can explore cities, complete missions, and engage in various activities (pretty violent).
  • Skyrim - An open world role playing game set in a fantasy universe where players can explore, complete quests, and develop their character. (always fun no matter what).
  • League of Legends - A multiplayer online battle arena (MOBA) game where players control champions and compete in team based matches (super team based).
  • Valorant - A tactical first person shooter that combines precise gunplay with unique character abilities in team based gameplay (think of Counter-Strike but you have special abilities).
  • Minecraft - Open-ended sandbox game that’s great for relaxing builds with friends.

When would I play these games?

Prompt used for above: “Make me an image that shows me playing my different video games at my desk!”

Lets first focus on the games I mentioned above. The Halo series was one of the first games I played growing up and I think that's where it started. It combined first person shooting with a great storyline where I could explore and be the hero. I then moved into games like Call of Duty, Grand Theft Auto, and Skyrim as I got older. These games were often more complex, you could explore more, and were generally closer to reality. Throughout middle and high school, I would play a lot of League of Legends and Valorant with my friends online. These were highly competitive games that focused on teamwork and strategy. I don't play any of these games anymore, but they were a big part of my life growing up and am looking forward to see if these games make a come back with new releases in the future.

The best times to play video depends on when I was growing up. From elementary school to middle school, the best time was practically anytime I wanted, and that meant I would play non-stop. As long as I didn't have homework, wasn't in school, didn't have a club meeting, or had something else to do ... I was probably playing Call of Duty of League of Legends during this time. When I was in highschool, I unfortunately played less and less due to the rigors of my schoolwork and community college. So I often played on the weekdays when I didn't have schoolwork, or on the weekends when I wasn't busy. Summer was typically the best time for me as a teenager because if I wasn't on vacation with my parents, or hanging out with my friends. Summer meant that when I was done working at my restaruant job, I could play video games until I was bored. Now that highschool is over, college is so demanding I rarely ever play, even in the summertime.

Now what video games do I play now? Honestly, that's a harder question to answer since I don't play nearly as much. I think recently I've played squad (a shooter based computer game), and minecraft with friends. These games are fun because they allow me to connect with my friends and that's typically what draws me to games the most now.

List of when I'd play video games

  • Elementary School: Anytime I wanted, played non-stop except when my parents would ground me ... haha
  • Middle School: After school and on weekends, school was super easy. As long as I didn't get grounded, I'd be okay.
  • High School: Weekends and summers, less during school year, school started becoming busy. I also worked and did school clubs.
  • College: Rarely play, occasional gaming with friends. I'm super focused on my studies now, but get to play a couple times a month maybe.
  • After Graduation: Hoping to carve out more time on weekends and during holidays.

Why I would play these games and the future!

There are so many more reasons that I play video games other than the ones that I mentioned in the first paragraph. Video games aren't only something for fun, but they're were something to keep me busy. They were a way for me to kill time, escape reality, challenge myself, and find something to do when I was bored. Being able to just keep my mind busy was a big reason why. No matter what emotion I was going through, video games were always there to help me get through it. Whether I was bored and needed something to do, sad and going through a breakup, or just needed to escape from reality for a little while, I always knew I could count on my video games to be there for me.

They are a part of my life and always will be, even if I rarely play anymore. In the future, I hope to explore different games, play with my friends like old times, and even explore virtual reality gaming. I think that is a super exciting and cool piece of technology that has so much potential. Overall, video games have been a great hobby of mine and I look forward to see what the future holds for gaming!
